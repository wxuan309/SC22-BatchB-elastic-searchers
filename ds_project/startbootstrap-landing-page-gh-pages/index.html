About

Our Product

This project will take the flight data you enter and use it to predict prices for airline tickets. Inputted data can include length, when the flight leaves, what time the flight leaves, and several other pieces of information.

Our Goal

Making travel plans can be difficult and expensive. When creating this project, we wanted to provide people with better planning when it comes to airline flights, whether that is organization or budgeting.

Our Process

To begin our project, we reviewed the data set and started off by removing the flight number, source and destination city. Instead of those, we added a distance column that was far more useful!

Understanding our Data

Our dataset consists of six different airlines. This pie chart visualizes the percentage of each airline present. We can clearly see that Vistara is almost half the data, while AirAsia is minor. This could potentially affect the predictions if your airline does not compare.

Days until Flight

This scatter plot shows a relationship between the number of days left to buy a ticket and their price. We can see that there tend to be significantly fewer flights just days before. It also tells us that Vistara appears to be a more expensive airline, while AirAsia is on the cheaper side.

Average Pricing

This bar graph shows the average pricing of each airline. Once again we can see Vistara as #1, followed by Air India. The others are not nearly as expensive but that could be due to our dataset favoring Vistara overall.

Time of Takeoff

When purchasing a flight, most people tend to care about what time it takes off and arrives at the destination. So the airline could use that to raise or lower the pricing. This graph shows if that is the case. It also shows how there are more flights in the evening opposed to the minuscule number of late night flights.

Understanding our Heatmap

This heatmap makes for easy understanding of how the flight characteristics affect each other. It does not only show how the price is changed, it shows if distance, days left to purchase, and duration are, or are not, directly connected.

Length Affecting Price

Our last scatter plot shows a correlation between the duration of a flight and its price. The duration of a flight is determined by the place you are traveling to, so of course it affects the price. But the airline you are traveling with affects the price even more. That is why Vistara always seems to be leading the price.

Machine Learning Models

Linear Regression

Linear Regression is a model that finds one line to best match all the data points. This was our starting point, but we realized that since our data has a huge range, this was not the most accurate of our models. Our recall score was 0.91.

Ridge Regression

Ridge Regressions are used on multicollinear data, meaning there are more than two correlated independent variables. Since it is multicollinear, there is a smaller chance of overfitting data. This model worked well with our data because multiple aspects of the flight affected the pricing. Our overall results for a ridge regression had an R-squared of 0.907.

Random Forest Regressor

Random Forest Regressors are sets of decision trees. Each input will go through our two-hundred individual trees; a price will be given based on the average output of the trees. This model worked well due to the complicated relationships between data points, and had an overall R-squared score of 0.978.

MLP Regressor

The Multi-Layer Perceptron is a ml model that uses a neural network to generate outputs by studying and refining past inputs. When using this to predict prices, we found that the data was too large for it to run. Unfortunately we were unable to get precise predictions with this model, the results only had a recall score of 0.819.

Conclusion

In terms of models, the random forest regressor is able to best predict flight prices; the several decision trees worked well with our dataset because multiple factors affected the outcomes. Besides that, we learned that the main factors when it comes to pricing is the airline and time of travel.

So What?

So, next time you are booking a flight, think of all the little factors, and try to predict the price for a better planning and budgeting experience!!
